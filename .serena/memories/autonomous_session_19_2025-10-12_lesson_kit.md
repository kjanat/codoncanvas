### Phase 3: Assessment Framework (30 min)

**File Created:** `ASSESSMENTS.md` (~450 lines)

**Structure:**

1. **Assessment Philosophy**: Conceptual understanding over memorization

2. **Pre-Assessment** (10 questions, before Lesson 1)
   - Purpose: Baseline knowledge
   - Topics: DNA structure, codons, mutations, prior experience
   - Format: Multiple choice + confidence rating
   - No grade, just data collection

3. **Formative Assessments** (during lessons)
   - Lesson 1 checkpoints: Codon recognition, family ID, creative application
   - Lesson 2 checkpoints: Mutation classification, nonsense recognition, frameshift understanding
   - Lesson 3 checkpoints: Transform application, creative synthesis, evolution reasoning
   - Purpose: Guide instruction, identify struggling students

4. **Post-Assessment** (after Lesson 3)
   - **Part A**: Conceptual (10 questions)
     - Codon families, mutation types, reading frames
     - Biological parallels (sickle cell example)
     - Transfer knowledge, evaluation
   - **Part B**: Practical (5 mutation identification challenges)
     - Given genome pairs, identify mutation type
     - Predict visual effects
   - **Part C**: Creative (open-ended synthesis)
     - Create original genome
     - Demonstrate all 4 mutation types
     - Document changes

5. **Scoring Rubrics**
   - Part A: Mastery (90-100%), Proficient (70-89%), Developing (50-69%), Beginning (<50%)
   - Part B: 0-2 points per challenge (type correct + effect correct)
   - Part C: Technical correctness (4pts) + Conceptual understanding (3pts) + Explanation (3pts)

6. **Learning Objectives Checklist**
   - Lesson-by-lesson objective tracking
   - Instructor can mark student progress

7. **Pilot Study Data Collection**
   - Quantitative: Time-to-first-artifact, retention, accuracy, satisfaction
   - Qualitative: Student feedback, instructor observations
   - Aligned with MVP success criteria

8. **Assessment Administration Guidelines**
   - When, how, purpose for each assessment
   - Student communication (no-pressure baseline)
   - Grading options (summative vs formative)

9. **Success Criteria** (Pilot Evaluation)
   - Individual: 20+ point improvement, 70%+ post-score, all 3 lessons complete
   - Program: 80%+ time-to-first <5min, 80%+ retention, 70%+ accuracy, 80%+ satisfaction

10. **Answer Keys**
    - Complete answers for pre, post, practical challenges
    - Enables consistent grading
